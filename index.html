What Is Algorithm Bias?

Algorithmic bias is systematic and repeatable errors in a computer system that create unfair outcomes, such as privileging one group of users over others.

History Of Algorithmic Bias

When it was first discovered and how it was discovered?

When and how was it discovered?

Algorithmic bias was discovered and created by Joseph Weizenbaum. In his 1976 book Computer Power and Human Reason, artificial intelligence pioneer suggested that bias could arise both from the data used in a program, but also from the way a program is coded.

Different types of algorithmic bias

There are two types of bias in AI. One is algorithmic AI bias or “data bias,” where algorithms are trained using biased data. The other kind of bias in AI is societal AI bias. That's where our assumptions and norms as a society cause us to have blind spots or certain expectations in our thinking.
